CONFIRMED PAPER TITLE
----------------------
"Capacitive and Inductive Near-Field Coupling of Electrically Small
 Antennas in TEM Cells via Equivalent Circuit Models"

CORE RESEARCH QUESTION
-----------------------
Bond wires in ICs act as parasitic electrically-short antennas (electric AND
magnetic dipoles) that radiate at fast signal rise times.
→ Standard fix = metallic shielding. But VCSELs need open apertures → shielding
  is not applicable.
→ Alternative: develop an equivalent circuit (EQC) to model the near-field
  coupling of electrically small structures to a TEM cell using lumped elements.
→ EQC advantage: reveals coupling mechanisms and is far faster than FEM.
→ Validate EQC against FEM results for monopole antenna and loop antenna.

--- CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION ---
Closely follows the introduction of Dominik Kreindl's paper (ACES2024_Kreindl_Official.pdf).

Key content:
- Motivation: shielding of a VCSEL device in an IC stripline environment is
  not possible, because the light of the device must pass through.
- A TEM cell is used for FEM investigations because its working principles
  are similar to those of the IC stripline (i.e., TEM wave propagation).
- Justification for EQC approach: the antenna is electrically small, so its
  near-field coupling to the TEM cell can be represented with lumped elements.
  → EQC provides physical insight into coupling mechanisms.
  → Circuit simulations are orders of magnitude faster than FEM.
- Brief explanation of why IC tests in IC striplines are always electrically short.
- Theory developed for the TEM cell geometry.
- Explanation of why electrically short devices can be effectively modeled
  with dipole moments (electric dipole moment me, magnetic dipole moment mm).

References: Dominik Kreindl's paper (ACES 2024), IEC 61967-8
See also: eqc.txt for detailed EQC formulations for both antenna types.


--- CHAPTER 2: TEM CELL MEASUREMENT ---
Based on the following references:
  [O1] T. Ostermann and B. Deutschmann, "TEM-Cell and Surface Scan to

       Proceedings of the 13th ACM Great Lakes symposium on VLSI, pp. 76–79, 2003.

Key content:

- Physical setup: DUT placed inside TEM cell, terminal voltages U1, U2
  measured via VNA at the two output ports.
- Dipole moment extraction equations:
    |me| = |U1 + U2| · h / ZW          [electric dipole moment]
    |mm| = |U1 − U2| · h · Z0 / ZW    [magnetic dipole moment]
  where h = septum–DUT distance, ZW = wave impedance (50 Ω), Z0 = 377 Ω.
- Why this approach is suitable for electrically short devices.

--- CHAPTER 3: EQUIVALENT CIRCUIT + RESULTS ---

3a. Equivalent circuit for the monopole antenna (capacitive coupling dominant):
- Antenna modeled as RLC series circuit (short electric dipole, Chu 1948).
  C_A dominates at low frequencies; L_A represents stored magnetic energy;
  R_A accounts for radiated power.
- Full circuit: antenna EQC extended with TEM cell model (L_T, C_T, symmetrically split).
- Coupling elements: C_k models electric coupling (displacement current → me);
  mutual inductances M_{A,T1} and M_{A,T2} model magnetic coupling (→ mm).
- For the monopole: mutual inductances negligible (M ≈ 0), C_k ≈ 1.68 pF.
- EQC parameters (C_A, L_A) derived from free-space FEM model via stored energy.

3b. Equivalent circuit for the loop antenna (both electric and magnetic coupling):
- Reduced EQC: R_A, L_A, C_A (ohmic loss and internal inductance neglected, PEC).
- Same extended circuit structure as monopole but with non-negligible M_{A,T1/2}.
  Both C_k (electric) and M (magnetic) coupling paths are active.
- C_A = 38.2 fF, L_A = 2.14 nH (from free-space FEM energy extraction).

3c. Dipole moment extraction from EQC (general two-step procedure):
- Step 1: identify source quantities — displacement current through C_k (→ me),
  induced voltages across L_{T1}, L_{T2} via mutual inductance (→ mm).
- Step 2: apply Kirchhoff's laws to find fraction of each source reaching R1, R2.
- Output voltages V'_{1,2} (magnetic) and V''_{1,2} (electric) superimposed.
- Modal wave amplitudes a_TEM, b_TEM computed → dipole moments via standard
  extraction equations.

3d. Phase difference:
- The PHASE DIFFERENCE between me and mm is the main indicator for the
  balance between electric and magnetic dipole moment magnitudes.
- Key physical insight and distinguishing feature of this EQC approach.

3e. Comparison FEM vs. EQC:
- Results for monopole antenna: me vs. frequency (EQC vs. FEM).
- Results for loop antenna: me and mm vs. frequency (EQC vs. FEM).
- EQC results derived using scripts in the /scripts folder:
    scripts/eqc-cap-antenna/   → monopole (capacitively coupled) EQC
    scripts/eqc-ind-antenna/   → loop (inductively coupled) EQC
    scripts/evaluate-moments/  → compute dipole moments over frequency
    scripts/generic-plotting/  → plot results

KEY EQUATIONS
-------------
|me| = |U1 + U2| · h / ZW
|mm| = |U1 − U2| · h · Z0 / ZW
  where: h = septum–DUT distance, ZW = 50 Ω, Z0 = 377 Ω

EQC output voltages (superposition):
  V'_{1,2}  = ±jωM·I_LA · [R_{1,2}||(1/jωC_{T1,2})] / [R_{1,2}||(1/jωC_{T1,2}) + jωL_{T1,2}]
  V''_{1,2} = (I_k/2) · (1/R_{1,2}) / (1/R_{1,2} + jωC_{T1,2})
